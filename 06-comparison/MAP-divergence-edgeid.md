## 1. The EdgeID-vs-Distance question, dissected (the core of the lens)

### 1.1 The two claims are about two different objects

| | EXT EdgeID (EXT §2, §14) | Our STE distance-uniqueness (REP §4.1, S1 §3) |
|---|---|---|
| **Object** | the EDGE's *identity token* | the EDGE's *displayed length* (Euclidean chord on the realized point set) |
| **Mechanism** | `H(PID_A,PID_B,BH_A,BH_B,C_A,C_B,q_A,q_B,e)` — a content hash over the full tuple | `Φ` (Sidon-Tower Embedding) → all `C(N,2)` pairwise distances measured distinct |

| **Scope** | every conceivable edge, infinite, by construction | the 627 behcs-256 anchor points (and, by M2, any finite realized lattice that re-passes QUANT-Δ) |
| **What it buys** | "I can always uniquely *name* an edge" | "the *picture* is faithful AND the *length* is itself a reverse-lookup key" |
| **What it does NOT buy** | does NOT make the *displayed distance* meaningful/non-degenerate | does NOT survive to infinity, and is NOT needed merely to name an edge |

These do not compete. A content hash `H(full tuple)` is collision-proof **whether or not** two edges share a Euclidean length. Conversely, distance-uniqueness is a fact about the *plot* (so that two lines do not overlap in length and the chord-length is itself invertible) that a hash does not provide and does not need. **EXT is right that distance can repeat in general; our report agrees and proves the conditions under which the displayed distance does NOT repeat on a realized set.**

### 1.2 Verdict: COMPLEMENT (with a recommended re-prioritization), not supersession

- **EXT does NOT get superseded by STE.** EdgeID is the safer *identity* primitive: it is finite-set-independent, infinite-by-construction, and immune to the pigeonhole limit that kills the naive linearizer. If the only question is "name the edge unambiguously," EdgeID wins outright and STE is unnecessary.
- **STE is NOT superseded by EdgeID.** EXT §2's own sentence — *"the graph SHOWS Euclidean distance visually… that is how the graph stays mathematically defensible"* — quietly *needs* something like STE the moment it makes any quantitative claim about the *plot* (centrality with no ties, novelty as a chord-length set-membership test, distance-as-a-name reverse lookup). Those are exactly our REP §3.9 / §4.1 payoffs, and they require the *displayed distance*, not the hash, to be injective. A hash gives you identity but throws away metric structure (a hash has no "distance"). So if you want the *geometry* to be a usable instrument (and both docs do — EXT's whole watcher stack reads the graph), you still need the STE-class result.
- **Therefore: COMPLEMENT.** EdgeID = identity layer (collision-proof naming). STE/QUANT-Δ = projection-fidelity layer (the displayed metric is injective on the realized set, so the picture is faithful and the length is a key). **Recommendation:** our report should adopt EXT's framing of *which layer is "the identity"* — make EdgeID/`line_id`/`signature` the canonical edge identity, and explicitly relabel STE as the *projection-fidelity certificate* rather than letting "distance-uniqueness" read as the identity guarantee.
